Concept Development

Next Office

A concept for how to create innovative and efficient office solutions including people, technology, and the physical environment. Based on research and analysis. To create sustainable office solutions, increase creativity, strengthen the brand, and for better health and well-being.

Background

Studies showed that the occupancy rate at the personal workstations was rarely more than 50%. The working life was undergoing several big changes. Mainly because of new technology, new values, and globalization. The traditional workplace didn‚Äôt support all the activities that occur in the office. Nor did these environments usually take into account the individual needs and driving forces of the employees. It was natural to look for new solutions that made more effective use of the workplace.

Scope

It started as external monitoring and ended up as a big project including a new sales process, showroom, new technology, and change management. We went from being a company selling products to also offer services. It was a great challenge for the salesforce who became consultants in areas they were not familiar with. But the concept was welcomed because it opened up to a whole new way of selling.

It is all about the analysis

Everything indicates that the workplace of the future is flexible, location-independent, and more activity-based. The workplace is configured in line with the actual workforce, it‚Äôs characterized by diversity and has more varied types of flexible, multi-functional, and stimulating environments to better meet the needs of the employees. The choice of environment is based on task, personality, age, physical needs, culture, mood, colleagues, design, etc. The personal desk is less important. One size doesn't fit all. An in-depth workplace analysis lays the foundation of a fully customized workplace.

MY ROLE: I took part in the development of the first version of the concept. My role wasn't clearly defined and changed along the way. Among others, I worked with development, project management, coordination, documentation, argumentation, packaging, and training material.
